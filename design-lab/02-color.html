FLEET ● DESIGN CONTRACT
02 — Color arriving is the information
No chips. Status is engraved — a glyph and a tinted label set directly on the black, separated by spacing and hairlines, never a container. A healthy screen is monochrome plus exactly one orange element. Green exists only as a decaying verified-moment tint. Direction of change in data is a separate, two-color affair — orange up, blue down — and direction is not status.

The rule — direction ≠ status
Orange = up, blue = down — arithmetic movement only. Waterfall increments and decrements, KPI deltas, period-over-period change. The pair judges nothing: a falling denial rate is blue because it fell, not because falling is bad.

Amber and red stay reserved. Behind and broken are statuses; they never mark direction, and the pair never marks status.

The ▲▼ glyph always rides along — direction survives without color.

One-thing-lit still governs chrome and status. At rest a screen keeps its single lit element; data ink inside a visual may use the pair freely.
